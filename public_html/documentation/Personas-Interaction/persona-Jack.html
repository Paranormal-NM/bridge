Name: Jack Sampson

Age: 73

Profession: Retired railroad Brakeman/Conductor. Lives with his wife in the same home where they raised their kids. He fills his time with family gatherings, game nights with friends, and railroad modeling with his friends.

Technology: Jack mainly uses a consumer-grade desktop computer running Windows with Chrome browser, as he doesn’t travel much outside his home. He is computer savvy enough to do what he needs and has an Android smartphone, both on the same company data plan.

Attitudes and Behaviors: Jack was raised an atheist, although he’s always had questions about the meaning of life and the nature of the universe. Atheism never made total sense to him, so he considers himself more an agnostic. As he ages, his mortality becomes prominent in his thoughts, especially after a recent health scare.

Frustrations and Needs: Having lived a full and rewarding life, Jack doesn’t have many frustrations or needs at this point. Among the minor annoyances are his somewhat diminished motor control that prevents him from doing the detailed work his model railroad demands from him. Not knowing what happens when we die is another source of frustration for him. He was never exposed any belief system that addressed it.

Goals: Jack visits ParanormalNM.com as a source of information that he’s been lacking his whole life: Where do we go when we die?

User Story: As a user, Jack is simply looking for validation that the spirit lives on after death. He’s looking for stories of Near-Death Experiences, and hopes that his fear of death will be assuaged by others’ encounters.

USER FLOW:

Jack has found ParanormalNM though Google

  • He reads across the navigation options and sees "Near-Death Experiences” which he clicks
  • The website serves a list of story titles from which to choose. Also detailed with the title is the Username of the author, the word count of the story, and the aggregate rating of the story. The most recently-posted story in that category is at the top of the list and is pre-loaded into the body.
  • After he has read the story, he selects another one from the list and it loads into the body of the website where he reads it.
  • After he finishes scrolling the story to the end, he sees several responses to the story that he also reads.
  • He repeats the procedure with a few more stories, then uses the search feature to find more stories about NDEs
